### Supplemental Table 10. Findings from studies of transportation insecurity and stage at diagnosis in colorectal cancer.

| Study | Outcome | Population | Sample | Exposure | Results | Summary |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Charlton 2016 [88]** | Stage at diagnosis | Iowa Medicare beneficiaries aged 65-84 diagnosed with colorectal cancer between 2002-2009 | 4,710 | Travel time to colonoscopy provider | *No significant results* | No association between travel time and stage at diagnosis. |
| **Crawford 2012 [89]** | Stage at diagnosis | All patients diagnosed with rectal or colon cancer between 1994-2002 in the Northern and Yorkshire Cancer Registry | Rectal: 7,058<br><br>Colon: 11,163 | Travel time by car from the patient's home to healthcare providers | *No significant results* | No effect of travel time on stage at diagnosis in either colon or rectal cancer. No interaction with area deprivation. |
| **Massarweh 2014 [90]** | Stage at diagnosis | US adults diagnosed with colon adenocarcinoma in the National Cancer Database between 2003-2010 | 296,474 | Point to point distance between patient and diagnosing facility | **aOR (95% CI)**<br>> 50 mi: 1.18 (1.12 - 1.24) | A distance of >50 miles to the diagnosing/treating facility was associated with an increased odds of metastatic disease at diagnosis after adjustment for potential confounders. |
| **Murage 2017 [91]** | Stage at diagnosis | People living in northern Scotland in the CRUX database diagnosed with colorectal cancer between 1997-1998 | 926 | Travel time from patient's home postcode to PCP postcode | *No significant results* | No association between travel time and stage at diagnosis in the adjusted model, nor in the model that included an interaction term between travel time and urban/rural status. |
| **Murage 2018 [77]** | Diagnosis via emergent presentation | Patients diagnosed with primary colorectal cancer in several cancer registries between 2006-2010 in England | 150,054 | Travel time to GP | **aRR (95% CI)**<br>20 - 30 min: 1.22 (1.08 - 1.37)<br>> 30 min: 1.39 (1.19 - 1.62) | Patients with colorectal cancer had significantly higher odds of diagnosis with an emergent presentation as travel time to PCP increased. |
| **Schlottmann 2020 [92]** | Stage at diagnosis | US adults diagnosed with colorectal cancer registered in the National Cancer Database between 2004-2015 | 361,187 | Point to point distance between patient and diagnosing and/or reporting facility | **aOR (95% CI)**<br>>18 mi: 1.20 (1.17 - 1.23) | The relationship between colorectal cancer stage at diagnosis and travel distance was variable, with most combinations of distance/stage showing no effect. There were increased odds of being diagnosed at Stage IV at the longest travel distances. |
| **Scoggins 2012 [79]** | Stage at diagnosis | Adults aged 18-64 enrolled in Medicaid and diagnosed with colorectal cancer in the Washington State Cancer Registry between 1997-2003 | 723 | Travel time to PCP | *No significant results* | Increasing travel time/distance traveled was not significantly associated with an increased stage at diagnosis. |
| **Virgilsen 2019 [87]** | Stage at diagnosis | Patients in the Danish Cancer Register (DCR) diagnosed with rectal or colon cancer between 2005-2016 | Rectal: 12,835<br><br>Colon: 25,257 | Distance to diagnosing hospital | **aOR (95% CI)**<br>Rectal > 62 km: 1.32 (1.10 - 1.59)<br><br>Colon: *No significant results* | Distances of >62km between patients and their diagnosing hospital are significantly associated with diagnosis at advanced tumor stage for rectal but not colon cancer. There was no association between distance between patients and their GP and advanced tumor stage at diagnosis for either colon or rectal cancer. |
